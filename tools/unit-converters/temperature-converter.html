Conversion Formulas
Celsius ↔ Fahrenheit

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

°C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

Celsius ↔ Kelvin

K = °C + 273.15

°C = K - 273.15

Fahrenheit ↔ Kelvin

K = (°F + 459.67) × 5/9

°F = (K × 9/5) - 459.67

Rankine Conversions

°R = °F + 459.67

°R = K × 9/5

Temperature Scales
  • Celsius (°C): Water freezes at 0°, boils at 100°
  • Fahrenheit (°F): Water freezes at 32°, boils at 212°
  • Kelvin (K): Absolute temperature scale, 0K = absolute zero
  • Rankine (°R): Absolute scale based on Fahrenheit degrees

Tips
  • -40°C equals -40°F (the only point where they're equal)
  • Kelvin never uses the degree symbol (°)
  • Absolute zero is the coldest possible temperature
  • Use Kelvin for scientific calculations
  • Celsius is used in most countries worldwide
